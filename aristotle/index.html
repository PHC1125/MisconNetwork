Aristotle's
Misconception Network

Weighted argumentation clusters: Soil · Water · Air · Light

M1 — Soil / Humus

Aristotle believed soil acts as an external stomach, pre-digesting and preparing nourishment for the plant's roots. Growth was seen as accretion of prepared earth-derived matter.

M2 — Water as Substance

Water was seen as the universal carrier of life. Van Helmont's willow experiment reinforced this — plant mass grew while visible soil barely changed, pointing to water as the source.

M3 — Air as Empty

Air was dismissed as weightless and incapable of building solid matter. Atmospheric carbon was entirely unintuitive — invisible substances could not explain a visible, heavy trunk.

M4 — Light as Mass

Because plants visibly depend on sunlight for growth, sunlight was treated as a material supplier rather than the energy that powers an internal chemical workshop.
