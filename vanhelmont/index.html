W — Willow Experiment

Van Helmont's famous 5-year willow experiment. A 5-lb willow grew to 169 lbs while 200 lbs of soil lost only 2 oz. He concluded water was transformed into all plant matter.

R — Rain Water Theory

Van Helmont believed rain water was pure and uncontaminated. Since only water was visibly added and the plant gained mass, pure water must be the material cause.

M — Mass Accounting

The experiment's fatal flaw was omitting atmospheric inputs. No attempt was made to weigh air or account for CO₂ absorption — the invisible input that actually explains most of the mass gain.

Integration

Water IS a reactant in photosynthesis, but the carbon framework of plant dry mass comes predominantly from atmospheric CO₂. Van Helmont's balance simply didn't weigh the air.
