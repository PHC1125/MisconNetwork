S — Salt Theory

Palissy proposed that mineral salts in soil are the true food of plants. Each plant seeks specific salts matched to its own composition — an early proto-theory of mineral nutrition.

F — Fertiliser & Ash

Palissy was a practical farmer. He observed that returning ash from burned straw restored field fertility — correctly identifying nutrient cycling but attributing the mass gain to salts rather than atmospheric carbon.

D — Soil Depletion

Palissy recognised that repeated cropping exhausts the soil, requiring fallowing, rotation, or fertilisation to restore productivity — a genuine insight into nutrient cycles.

Integration

Palissy was right that mineral salts are essential — but wrong that they are the primary source of plant mass. Carbon from atmospheric CO₂ builds the bulk of dry matter; minerals are micronutrients.
